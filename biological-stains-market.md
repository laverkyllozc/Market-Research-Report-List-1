<p><p>The biological stains market includes various types utilized in microscopy and histology for visualizing cellular components. Acid Fuchsin is used for staining connective tissues, while Congo Red binds to beta-pleated sheets, highlighting amyloid deposits. Crystal Violet is effective for Gram staining in bacteria. Eosin provides contrast in tissue sections, and Methylene Blue is primarily used in cell biology. Iodine highlights starch in plant tissues, and Safranin often serves as a counterstain in histological preparations. Other stains cater to specific applications and research needs.</p></p>
