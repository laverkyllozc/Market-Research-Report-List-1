<p><p>The commercial aircraft airframe materials market comprises four primary types: aluminum alloys, titanium alloys, composites, and steel alloys. Aluminum alloys are prized for their lightweight and corrosion resistance, making them ideal for structure and skin applications. Titanium alloys offer superior strength and heat resistance, essential for high-stress components. Composites, including carbon fiber, provide exceptional strength-to-weight ratios and design flexibility. Steel alloys, while heavier, are used for specific high-strength applications due to their durability and cost-effectiveness. These materials drive innovation in aerospace engineering.</p></p>
