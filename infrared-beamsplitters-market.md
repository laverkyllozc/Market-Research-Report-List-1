<p><p>The infrared beamsplitters market comprises various types based on material composition. Calcium fluoride (CaF2) is known for its wide transmission range and low absorption, making it suitable for infrared applications. Potassium bromide (KBr) offers excellent transmittance in the mid-infrared range but is more sensitive to moisture. Zinc selenide (ZnSe) provides good optical properties and durability, particularly in high-power lasers. Other materials may include germanium and silicon, each chosen for specific applications depending on their transmission characteristics and environmental stability.</p></p>
