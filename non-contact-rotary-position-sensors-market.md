<p><p>Non-contact rotary position sensors are vital in various industrial applications, offering two primary output types: digital and analog. Digital output sensors provide a binary signal indicating positions as discrete values, making them ideal for systems requiring precise on-off control. Conversely, analog output sensors generate a continuous signal that represents the position within a range, allowing for smoother and more nuanced control. Both types enhance performance, reduce wear, and improve reliability in applications where accurate rotary measurements are crucial.</p></p>
