<p><ul><li>Branch Retinal Vein Occlusion</li><li>Central Retinal Vein Occlusion</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The retinal vein occlusion therapeutics market is primarily categorized into two types: Branch Retinal Vein Occlusion (BRVO) and Central Retinal Vein Occlusion (CRVO). BRVO affects smaller branches of the retinal vein, often leading to localized vision impairment and requiring treatments like anti-VEGF injections or corticosteroids. CRVO, impacting the main retinal vein, typically results in more severe vision loss and may necessitate advanced therapies such as laser treatments or intravitreal injections. Both conditions require tailored approaches to restore vision and manage symptoms.</p></p>
