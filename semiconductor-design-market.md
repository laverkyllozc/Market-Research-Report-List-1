<p><ul><li>FPGA</li><li>ASIC</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The semiconductor design market primarily includes two key types: FPGA (Field-Programmable Gate Array) and ASIC (Application-Specific Integrated Circuit). FPGAs are versatile and can be reprogrammed for various tasks, making them ideal for prototyping and flexibility. In contrast, ASICs are custom-designed for specific applications, offering optimized performance, power efficiency, and smaller size but lack reprogramming capability. Both markets cater to diverse industries, with FPGAs favoring rapid development and adaptability, while ASICs excel in high-volume production and specialized tasks.</p></p>
