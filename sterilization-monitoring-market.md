<p><ul><li>Biological Monitoring</li><li>Chemical Monitoring</li><li>Mechanical Monitoring</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The sterilization monitoring market comprises three main types: biological, chemical, and mechanical monitoring. Biological monitoring uses live spores to verify sterilization effectiveness, ensuring that equipment renders pathogens inactive. Chemical monitoring involves the use of indicators that change color or appearance to confirm that certain sterilization parameters were met. Mechanical monitoring assesses the physical conditions of sterilization equipment, such as temperature and pressure, through built-in gauges and recording devices. Each type plays a crucial role in ensuring effective sterilization practices in various industries.</p></p>
